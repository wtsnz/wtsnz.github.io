About

I drink a lot of tea, I like to code and when I'm in the right mood I enjoy design/art. I've recently been toying with a couple of languages that can be used to make web applications - Ruby, Go and Node.js. Which I've found to be a lot of fun and may help to bring some of my wacky product ideas to life.

I'm also into game design (who isn't), which is how it all started for me back when flash games were a thing. I've got a few prototype games that I've got on my iPhone that I must get around to releasing. So watch this space.

I've started to play guitar, after getting inspired by Ed Sheeran. Maybe one day I'll try and sing. what.

A Brief History

I was made in England around Jan 1992 and have been living in New Zealand since 2006. I aspired to become somebody in the film/visual effects industry (hence my twitter handle @vfxguynz) and uploaded a few videos to my youtube channel. To my suprise one video has 100,000+ views! I lost interest in the 3D Animation course I attended as I started creating games for the HP Touchpad (and won the HP WebOS Weekly App Hack!).

After that, I decided to go to Auckland University and try my luck at a CompSci Degree. I lived about an hours ferry journey away, so I decided to purchase a Mac Book Pro because the battery life was much better than most competing laptops and would last me the day.

This has ended up becoming one of the most pivotal moments of my life so far, as buying that MacBook enabled me to download xcode. After playing around with the Cocoa Touch SDK on the ferry, I got the idea for my first app, Tides NZ and managed to get the first version released during the first two week uni break. It went of to be a better success that I could have imagined and solidified my decision to become a software engineer.

Soon after meeting up with a few developers in Auckland, through word of mouth I landed an iOS contract down in Wellington for a couple of weeks which has lead into my current full time position at PaperKite.

I've was also lucky enough to be selected for Time Magazine's Person of the Year in 2006.
